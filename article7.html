Best Planting Times for Popular Flowers

Discover the ideal seasonal windows for planting favourite flowers, from spring bulbs to warm-weather annuals. This guide helps home gardeners time their planting for stronger blooms and a thriving garden throughout the year.

Tulips, daffodils, and crocus prefer autumn planting for spring display, while summer favourites like marigolds, petunias, and zinnias are best started in late spring after danger of frost has passed.

Perennials such as lavender, coneflowers, and salvia establish best when planted in early spring or early fall, giving roots time to settle before heat or winter arrives.

Use this timing guide alongside local weather cues to ensure each flower is planted when the soil is workable and temperatures are stable. Most importantly, match planting time with the plant’s natural growth cycle for healthier, more resilient blooms.
