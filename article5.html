Data Design: The Story Behind the Page

This story looks at how editorial designers use data and visual systems to create compelling magazine storytelling. We focus on the craft of turning research, charts, and observations into pages that feel intentional, readable, and beautiful.

The article highlights editorial strategy, layout rhythm, and the importance of giving every piece of data a clear place in the story. It also reviews how colour, space, and typography work together to guide readers.

By combining journalistic curiosity with visual discipline, magazines can turn data into a memorable narrative that supports both context and clarity.
